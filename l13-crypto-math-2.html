Group theory review

(Multiplicative groups)

(G, *) is a finite abelian group of size t if:

  • \exists identity 1 s.t. \forall a \in G a*1 = 1*a = a
  • \forall a \in G, \exists b \in G s.t. a*b = 1
  • \forall a,b,c \in G, a*(b*c) = (a*b)*c
  • \forall a,b \in G, a*b = b*a

Definition: The order of a in G is order(a) = least u s.t. a^u = 1

Lagrange's theorem: In a finite abelian group of size t, for all a \in G, order(a) | t

Theorem: In a finite abelian group of size t, \forall a \in G, a^t = 1

Example: a^(p-1) = 1, \forall a \in Z*_p because |Z*_p| = 1
